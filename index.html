DSPDX Website Redesign

Our team was tasked with redesigning the website of a non-profit local to Portland, OR. The success of which is measured by our redesigns ability to effectively solve for navigation, content hierarchy, and usability deficiencies. Additionally, our redesign needs to consider responsive web design practices via components, elements, and site layouts that dynamically adjust

Research

1. Understand the methods utilized by users when accessing socially motivated web-based content as well as said contents discovery 2. Understand the most common user practices for connecting directly with non-profit orgs 3. What are the core offerings, characteristics, and features of an information-sharing based website users expect/are motivated by

In order to gain insight into our above objectives, we anticipate conducting a total of 5 user interviews. Our interviewees will be sourced via local connections adhering approximate demographics as represented by our proto-persona, Dax Joseph. Each interview will include a total of fifteen questions and should run for no longer than 30 minutes. As our interviews will comprise the majority of the primary research we conduct, each interview transcript will be utilized by the team when forming an affinity diagram to generate a more developed persona and user insights.

Problem Statement

As a team of researchers, we want to understand how users are most compelled to engage with website based content that disseminates information pertaining to social issues by a non-profit organization as they are currently leveraging social media effectively.

Testing/Iterations

Colors and textures are inspired by the history of black activism and mitual aid programs. Buttons and Icons pull from the color palette and are simple in design, taking a backseat to the content of the site.

Our typography was heavily infulenced by the Don’t Shoot PDX logo. We wanted a type face that mirrored the bold and punchy serif font from the logo and spoke to the content that Don’t Shoot shares. For our secondary font we chose a sanserif font that was readable and had a variety of weights we could use to create a clear hierarchy throughout a very text heavy site. Considering all the articles, resources, posts, and information that Don’t shoot shares with their users.

We wanted to present the main content on the site with a bulletin board meets social media asethetic. With an elevated interactice tile that presents the user with individualized interactions based on the type of content that is being presented. ei. Add to Calendar functions for events, and Read More functions for Articles and Blogs. Using a dark semi-opaque overlay with white text to ensure readability for the users over a variety of content, wether it be photos, graphics, or text heavy posters. Featuring bright white and red interactive buttons to draw users in to the functionality of the card.
